What Techletter is, and who it's for.

Techletter is an independent newsletter on AI, written for the people who have to make sense of it: policymakers, governance and compliance leaders, legal and regulatory teams, researchers, founders, and decision-makers. It follows AI across four fronts, governance and regulation, business and strategy, responsible use, and the impact of AI on society, and turns primary sources into reading you can act on. The cheat sheets and reports in the library are the same work, distilled into references you can keep.

A consultant and researcher on responsible AI.

Nesibe Kırış Can advises organizations on Responsible AI, regulatory readiness, and governance frameworks, and designs executive training on AI ethics and AI strategy. Her work sits where technology, policy, and society meet.

She is co-author of several AI governance studies, including work on global indices, ISO 42001 implementation, and civil society AI literacy. As a Certified AI Governance Professional (AIGP), she has completed executive programs at Oxford Saïd Business School, Anthropic, the Alan Turing Institute, and BlueDot Impact, and holds degrees in Law and Sociology from Koç University.

She founded Techletter to make AI governance legible, and consults across fintech, crypto, AI, healthtech, and platform sectors. Training is delivered online in English and Turkish. To work together, write to me@nesibekiris.com.

Questions & answers

Common questions about the reports and cheat sheets.

What is the Techletter reports and cheat sheets library?
It is the companion library to Techletter: the cheat sheets, reports, and AI Wrapped editions published alongside the newsletter, collected in one place at techletter.co/reports so they are easy to find and use. Techletter is an independent newsletter on AI covering governance and regulation, business and strategy, responsible use, and AI's impact on society, read by over 9,800 people across 90+ countries.
How do I access the Techletter cheat sheets and reports?
The library is part of Techletter. Resources are released through the newsletter and available to subscribers. Subscribe at techletter.co to access the cheat sheets and reports and to receive new editions first.
What topics does Techletter cover?
Techletter covers AI across four areas: governance and regulation (such as the EU AI Act, ISO 42001, and the NIST AI RMF), business and strategy, responsible use and real-world use cases, and the impact of AI on society. You will find cheat sheets and reports on all four in the library.
Who writes Techletter, and are the resources reliable?
Techletter is written by Nesibe Kırış Can, a consultant and researcher specializing in AI governance, technology policy, and responsible innovation, and a Certified AI Governance Professional (AIGP). Every resource cites primary sources such as EUR-Lex, ISO, NIST, and national regulators, with dates and version numbers so you can check how current it is.
What is an AI Wrapped edition?
AI Wrapped is Techletter's yearly review of the year in AI, across governance and regulation, business, responsible use, and society. It summarizes the shifts that mattered most and what they mean for the year ahead.
How often is the library updated?
New cheat sheets and reports are published as regulation and standards change, usually every month. The most recent updates were the ISO 42001 cheat sheet and The Complete AI Policy Outline in July 2026.
How can I work with Nesibe on consulting or training?
Nesibe offers three service lines. AI Maturity & Governance: maturity assessments and diagnostics, strategic roadmaps and operating models, governance frameworks and policies (built with the R.A.I.L.W.A.Y. method), and regulatory readiness and standards alignment. AI4Whom Training: practical AI ethics and governance programs for every level, from frontline teams (AI4Everyone) to executive strategy (AI4Strategy, RAI as ROI). Policy Consultancy: public policy advisory, government affairs, and policy research across fintech, crypto, AI, healthtech, and platform sectors. She is based in Istanbul and delivers training online in English and Turkish. To work together, write to me@nesibekiris.com.
